# Risk Register Activity – Risk Assessment and Prioritization

## Project Overview

This project documents a risk register exercise used to identify, assess, and prioritize cybersecurity risks for an organization. The goal was to capture key threats, evaluate likelihood and impact, and outline mitigation strategies aligned with business priorities.

A structured risk register helps security teams track risk ownership, prioritize remediation, and communicate exposure clearly to stakeholders.

---

## Objectives

- Identify relevant cybersecurity risks
- Evaluate likelihood and impact
- Assign severity and priority levels
- Recommend mitigation and response strategies
- Document ownership and next steps

---

## Methodology

The risk register was built using a standard qualitative approach:

1. **Risk Identification** – List potential threats and weaknesses
2. **Impact Analysis** – Assess potential business, operational, and regulatory impact
3. **Likelihood Estimation** – Evaluate probability based on existing controls and exposure
4. **Risk Rating** – Assign a severity level based on likelihood and impact
5. **Mitigation Planning** – Define corrective actions and responsible owners

---

## Risk Register Summary

The full register is provided in the attached PDF. Key findings include:

- High-risk items linked to access control gaps and lack of encryption
- Medium-risk items tied to monitoring and process weaknesses
- Low-risk items related to physical security and minor policy gaps

These findings were prioritized based on business impact and exposure.

Risk registers are essential for translating technical security issues into actionable business priorities. This exercise demonstrates how structured risk documentation supports decision-making, resource allocation, and strategic security planning.
